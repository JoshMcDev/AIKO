C-8 Common Concerns for Each Methodology

It is important to understand and consider the benefits and possible down-sides of each approach in order to ensure you select the one that will help you achieve best value for the customer/program. Below are some of the common concerns.

Needs At the Price Proposed?

The government shall incorporate evaluated strengths as a contractually binding requirement to the greatest extent possible (particularly when offeror was selected under VATEP).

Post-award management must follow through to ensure receipt of the anticipated benefits.

The government sometimes has difficulty identifying with enough clarity and specificity what its requirements are (even when we think we’ve done a good job).

If this occurs, the contract may require modifications to ensure the government’s needs are met, which may increase the price over time.

Thorough, upfront analysis is essential. Careful post-award management is equally as important.
